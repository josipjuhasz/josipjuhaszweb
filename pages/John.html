John Frusciante

John Frusciante, (born 5 March 1970) is an American musician, singer, songwriter, and producer best known as the lead guitarist of the rock band Red Hot Chili Peppers across three stints (1988–1992, 1998–2009, 2019–present). He has recorded five studio albums with them. Frusciante has an active solo career, having released twelve solo albums and seven EPs; his recordings include elements ranging from experimental rock and ambient music to new wave and electronica.

Some interesting facts

  • Frusciante has received critical recognition for his guitar playing, ranking at number 18 on Rolling Stone's list of "The 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time" in 2003
  • Frusciante released six solo albums in 2004; each album explored different recording techniques and genres.
  • He credits his inspiration for learning guitar to Greg Ginn, Pat Smear, and Joe Strummer, amongst others.
